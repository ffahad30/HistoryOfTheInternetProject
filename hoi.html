History of the Internet

The Internet is a collection of multiple different pieces of computer and non-computer equipment talking to one another by means of a communications network either wired or wireless.

Oct. 4, 1957 - Russia puts Sputnik into orbit

Feb 7, 1958 - Space exploration assigned to ARPS

Oct 1, 1958 - NASA opens for business via Eisenhower executive order

July 1961 - Leonard Kleinrock of MIT wrote the first paper on packet switching theory

August 1962 - J.C.R. Licklider of MIT - memos on “Galactic Network Concept”

October 1969 - Larry Roberts heads project that connects first two ARPANET sites

October 1972 - Bob Kahn demonstrates ARPANET at the ICCC

March 1972 - Ray Tomlinson writes first basic email program using “@”

May 1974 - Vinton Cerf & Ray Kahn produce A Protocol for Packet Network Interconnection

1978 - TCP and IP split

1980 - Tim Berners-Lee - writes predecessor to WWW

Nov 1982 - Jon Postel, Paul Mockapedia, and Craig Partridge design DNS

1990 - Tim Berners-Lee creates World Wide Web

1993 - Marc Andreesen develops Mosaic web browser

1996 - Microsoft vs. Netscape Browser Wars
